# Employee to CEO

Use this skill to transform a client's current experience into a staged ownership plan. Do not treat business formation alone as becoming a CEO. Require evidence of revenue ability, repeatable operations, financial control, and delegation.

## Core workflow

1. Establish the client's current state:
   - job and transferable experience
   - location and service territory
   - available vehicle, tools, licenses, certifications, and capital
   - current income, monthly obligations, credit profile, and available hours
   - sales comfort, technology comfort, and management experience
   - target income and deadline

2. Select the lowest-friction path to first revenue. Rank options by:
   - speed to qualify
   - startup cost
   - local demand
   - expected gross margin
   - regulatory burden
   - dependence on one platform
   - ability to win direct contracts
   - ability to hire and delegate later

3. Build the skill-acquisition ladder:
   - operator skill: perform the service reliably
   - revenue skill: prospect, quote, sell, and close
   - business skill: price, contract, insure, invoice, and keep records
   - systems skill: document procedures and track performance
   - leadership skill: recruit, train, delegate, and manage
   - CEO skill: allocate capital, review metrics, manage risk, and expand

4. Produce a phased transition:
   - Phase 1 — Employee to Skilled Operator
   - Phase 2 — Skilled Operator to Independent Contractor
   - Phase 3 — Contractor to Business Owner
   - Phase 4 — Owner to CEO

5. Include a 30/60/90-day plan with weekly actions, measurable milestones, required documents, estimated costs, and stop/go decision points.

6. Separate immediate-income opportunities from long-term ownership opportunities. Explain when gig platforms are useful for proof of performance but insufficient as the final business model.

7. Recommend the next TAG 9 service only when it logically follows from the diagnosis.

## Required output

Return these sections:

1. **Executive diagnosis** — current stage, strongest assets, main constraints, and recommended path.
2. **Opportunity ranking** — top three paths with rationale and risk.
3. **Skills to acquire** — ordered by urgency, with a practical method to acquire each skill.
4. **Business foundation** — entity, banking, insurance, bookkeeping, contracts, compliance, and pricing requirements.
5. **30/60/90-day roadmap** — weekly tasks and measurable outcomes.
6. **Revenue ladder** — first paid work, recurring clients, direct contracts, and delegation target.
7. **CEO scorecard** — revenue, gross margin, cash reserve, client concentration, on-time performance, sales pipeline, documented SOPs, and delegated workload.
8. **Risk controls** — regulatory, financial, operational, and concentration risks.
9. **Next action** — the single most important action to complete within 48 hours.

## Guardrails

- Do not promise income, financing, contracts, licenses, or approval.
- Distinguish estimates from verified facts.
- Use current sources before stating live opportunities, regulations, pricing, or platform requirements.
- Do not recommend debt before testing demand and basic unit economics.
- Do not call a client a CEO merely because an LLC was filed.
- Prefer one clear path over an unfocused list of unrelated businesses.
- Flag when a licensed attorney, accountant, insurance professional, or regulated-industry specialist is needed.
