# CEO Stage Model

## Stage 1 — Employee
The person exchanges time for wages and follows another organization's system.

Evidence to advance:
- one monetizable skill identified
- time and financial constraints documented
- target market selected

## Stage 2 — Skilled Operator
The person can deliver a paid result independently and consistently.

Evidence to advance:
- service standards defined
- proof of work or completed jobs
- basic pricing and cost estimate
- customer feedback or performance record

## Stage 3 — Independent Contractor
The person can find and serve clients under their own responsibility.

Evidence to advance:
- business structure appropriate to the activity
- insurance and compliance addressed
- invoicing and recordkeeping working
- at least one repeatable lead source

## Stage 4 — Business Owner
The person owns a repeatable system that produces revenue beyond a single shift or gig.

Evidence to advance:
- recurring or contracted revenue
- documented SOPs
- operating metrics
- cash reserve and tax discipline
- reduced dependence on one customer or platform

## Stage 5 — CEO
The person directs strategy, capital, people, risk, and growth rather than personally performing every task.

Evidence:
- work is delegated without service failure
- management rhythm and scorecard exist
- hiring and training system exists
- client concentration is controlled
- expansion decisions use unit economics and territory data

## Scoring rule

Score each dimension from 0 to 5:
- Revenue generation
- Sales pipeline
- Financial control
- Compliance
- Operations
- Documentation
- Delegation
- Leadership
- Risk management
- Growth readiness

Interpretation:
- 0–10: employee stage
- 11–20: skilled operator
- 21–30: independent contractor
- 31–40: business owner
- 41–50: CEO-ready
